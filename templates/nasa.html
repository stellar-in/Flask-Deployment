Information about exoplanets

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. The first confirmed exoplanet was discovered in 1992, and thousands have since been identified using methods like transit, radial velocity, and direct imaging. They vary in size and composition, including gas giants, rocky planets, and ice giants. Some are in the "habitable zone," where liquid water might exist, suggesting potential for life.
